mkgrid — a privacy-first Instagram grid planner

mkgrid is a free, open-source Instagram grid planner that runs entirely in your browser. Plan and preview your Instagram feed before you post: drag in your photos, arrange the grid, crop each tile with pan and zoom, and span a single image across a full row for panorama or banner posts. mkgrid uses Instagram's real 3:4 thumbnail ratio, with 4:5 and 1:1 options for legacy content. Export post-ready images as a ZIP, or export your entire profile as one image to preview the whole feed. Everything happens locally on your device — no upload, no account, no cookies, no analytics, and no tracking. Your photos never leave your browser. mkgrid is a single static HTML file you can read, fork, and self-host under the MIT license.

TIPS
  • Span a photo like a collage — tap SPAN on a row to stretch one image across all three tiles.
  • Export your work — hit EXPORT to save every tile as a ready-to-post image, or your whole feed as one picture.
  • Keyboard shortcuts — Ctrl Z / Ctrl Y to undo / redo.
  • Rearrange — grab a tile's ⠿ handle and drag it to a new spot.
  • Reorder rows — on desktop, grab the ⠿ handle on the left of any row and drag it up or down.
  • Reframe a photo — drag inside it to move the crop; mouse wheel or pinch to zoom. On a trackpad, hold Ctrl while scrolling.
FAQ
Do my photos stay private?

Yes — completely. mkgrid runs entirely in your browser. There's no server, no upload, no account. Your images never leave your device.

Do I need an account?

No signup, no login, no email. Open it and start planning.

Is it really free?

Yes — free and open-source (MIT). No ads, no upsells, nothing behind a paywall.

Where is my work saved?

Locally, in your browser — on your device, never a server. Clearing your browser data removes it.

Why a 3:4 grid?

Instagram now crops grid thumbnails to 3:4, so mkgrid matches it by default. Switch to 4:5 or 1:1 anytime.

Is it open source?

Yes — one readable file. Read or fork it on GitHub ↗
